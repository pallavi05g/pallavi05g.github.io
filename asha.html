Asha Concept
Self Sustained Environment and Waste Segregation Awareness Program

Project Description

The notion behind Asha was to promote civic sense and environmental responsibility and foster innovative ways to inculcate the awareness about waste segregation and management solutions.

Project Details

  • When: Sophomore year | R V College of Engineering | 2012
  • Where: Sustainable Energy Challenge 2012(SEC 2012) organised by SELCO at Indian Institute of Management Bangalore(IIM-B) - Finalist
  • Role: Conceptualization | Sketching | Brainstorming | Presentation
  • Project Type: Team of 2
  • Time frame: 4 weeks

Problem

Asha - Sapno Ki Jholi was born when we started discussing about the energy crisis in the city and how we could make a difference. In Hindi, "Asha" means hope and "Sapno Ki Jholi" means a cradle of dreams. This project was an effort in the direction of raising awareness about waste management.

Target Audience

Children in rural India or urban slums who would engage with this solution via different activities based on age

Our Proposal

The solution that we developed comprised of the various components, which would together result in a cost effective solution. The whole solution was designed keeping in mind rural India as the target audience.



A. User friendly application in the form of a game which would teach children about the various aspects of waste management;

B. Fact randomiser which would deal with concepts related to civic sense, waste disposal, segregation;

C. Picture filled presentations that would cover harmful effects of dumping and inefficient waste disposal;

D. Newsletter that would demonstrate segregation success stories, community involvement and benefits and 3R (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle) bins

Process Diagram

Process

A. Framing the problem: We identified that energy crisis and waste management was the need of the hour in and around the city of Bangalore. Various press reports and media channels were discussing the alarming threat on the city's environment and impact on health.

B. Exploring the solution space: We identified potential solutions after discussing with people in the NGO sector, environmentalists and folk in public health space. This stage involved brainstorming and sketching.



C. Detailing the solutions identified: We detailed out every solution that we had identified earlier and how it would solve the problem that we were trying to address. This stage involved more sketching and storyboarding.



D. Refining our solution: We gathered feedback about our proposal and created a video explaining the project, target audience, intended audience, solution and impact.



As finalists, we presented our proposal at Indian Institute of Managament, Bangalore to a panel of environmentalists, NGOs, media, government officials in the public health and energy sector, community and startups at Sustainable Energy Challenge 2012(SEC 2012) organised by SELCO Foundation.
