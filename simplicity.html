Simpl-i-city Interaction Design
One-stop solution for any newbie in a city

Description

Simpl-i-City is a one-stop solution for any newbie in a city (food, local lingo, places to see, clubs, stay etc). This prototype aims to eliminate the hurdles faced with respect to local food, language, places to explore, clubs, places to stay. This prototype was developed as a part of the Human Computer Interaction MOOC by Scott Klemmer.

Details

  • When: April 2014
  • Where: Coursera (MOOCs)
  • Role: Interaction Designer
  • Project Type: Individual
  • Time frame: 3 weeks

Problem

Today traveling has become so easy that people tend to travel frequently for work or vacation. On arriving at a new place, an individual is curious about the place and wants to learn more. While a search on the Internet reveals information about the place, information about local lingo, best off-beat locations within the city and local favorites require a longer search. The theme behind this project is Change and to understand how change effects individuals and what they aspire to know when they move to a new place.

When I was working on this project, I did not have adequate experience with respect to moving to a new city. However, a lot of people moving into the city used to ask me a lot of questions that were pretty much common. My entire solution was based on the user interviews and observations. Today. I can relate to this problem as I stay roughly 8000 miles (13000 km) away from home.

Process

Target Audience

Curious travellers who want to experience the true local spirit of the place

Any one who moves to a new place

Target Solution

A high-fidelity prototype that was developed as a part of this MOOC by Scott Klemmer using standard templates given.

Problem scoping

This stage involved interviewing users who understood the challenges faced when moving to a new city either for work or study and curious travellers who want to understand the local culture of a place. Some of the problems that were highlighted in the user interviews -

  • Navigating through a lot of information online and trying to make sense of it
  • Sparse Information with respect to specific details that they were seeking for
  • Information about local hangout places, lingo, must-eat non-cliched places



    • Exploring the solution space

    This stage involved brainstorming individually and with peers to understand user needs and gather insights.

  • User X was not aware of the local language of the city he migrated to. It was getting harder by the day to stay in that city
  • User Y stayed in a region that had severe rules were imposed and major search engines were blocked.
  • User Z wished to learn local lingo quickly for easy navigation and partying in the city

    • Finding a good solution

    This stage involved storyboarding, brainstorming and design critique sessions and persona creation.



    Low Fidelity Mockups

    This stage involved creating mockups and wireframes. I used Balsamiq Mockups to create my low-fi prototype. I created a mockup for mobile devices and desktops.
