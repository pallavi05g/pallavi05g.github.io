Surface Touch
Tangible Interface to control devices using taps

Surface Touch Interaction Design

Project Description

Project Details

  • When: Junior year | R V College of Engineering | 2012
  • Where: MIT Media Lab Design Innovation Workshop 2013 | PES Institute of Technology, Bangalore
  • Role: Interaction Designer
  • Project Type: Team of 5
  • Time frame: 5 days

Problem

We wanted to create an interface that would allow people to interact with a system naturally and since users are familiar with taps and claps, we decided on base on project on that

Target Solution

A tangible user interface developed using Processing and piezoelectric sensors that converts any non-digitized surface into a surface that listens to a user by identifying unique acoustic gesture inputs





Our Poster



Process

This tangible user interface prototype was developed over a period of 5 days in a team of 5 during the MIT Media Lab Design Innovation workshop.



On the first day, we focused on identifying a problem by touring in the city. We took down notes and later brainstormed the pros and cons of each of our ideas. It was really interesting to evaluate an idea holistically and find like-minded inidividuals as team members. I met 4 others from Design and Computer Science backgrounds.





On the second day, we brainstormed and conducted feasibility studies and pitched our ideas to the entire cohort. The feedback given by other teams during the usability studies was invaluable.





The following two days, we were involved in intense rapid prototyping. The final prototype was written in Processing and used Piezo Electric sensors.





Working under Media Lab researchers, Daniel Leithinger and Anirudh Sharma was incredible. The workshop concluded with a project open house and a series of talks by various folks from MIT Media Lab including Ramesh Raskar, where they discussed several aspects of design and programming. Amazing hosts, brilliant mentors, splendid talks, delicious food, inspiring TEDX talks, and new friends, added to the sheer marvel of the entire workshop, making it one of the most memorable experiences of my life.
