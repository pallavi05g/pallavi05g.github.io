STOReeFY
Interactive Storytelling through gamification of cultural folklore

STOReeFY Interaction Design Project

Project Description

STOReeFY introduces an interactive way of listening to age-old stories.

  • Wide collection of interactive stories to choose from
  • Curated story albums created from in-game tasks within the interactive story
  • Popular stories based on what your peers are engaging with

Project Details

  • When: Sept 2016 to Present
  • Where: University of Michigan, Ann Arbor
  • Role: Interaction Designer
  • Project Type: Individual
  • Time frame: 12 weeks

Problem

With the advent of technology, there is a widening generation gap that is observed around the world. Right from early years, children are exposed to popular culture and media that is resulting in neglect towards culture awareness. Cultural folklore and tales are limited to places of cultural interest or school events. Age-old storytelling that was a means of mutual bonding is no longer seen. Due to relatively few topics in common, conversations are non-existent between tech-challenged grandparents and folklore-challenged grandkids.

Target Audience

Young kids and older adults

Essentially anyone eager to have fun, learn about folklore and tales

Target Solution

In order to ensure a shared mutual experience between older folk and kids, instill cultural awareness in kids and familiarize older generation with technology, Storeefy introduces interactive storytelling through gamification of cultural folklore

Features

  • Rich collection of stories to choose from
  • In-game activities and rewards to keep you engaged
  • Story Moments to see how you aced the game
  • Personal story albums that you can share with your friends and family
  • Get custom results using our search and filters feature
  • Popular Stories section to see what your peers are engaging with
  • Recommendations based on your interests
  • History section to view how many stories you have learnt over time

Process Diagram

Problem scoping

This stage involved user research and competitor analysis.





    Exploring the solution space

    This stage involved brainstorming individually and with peers to understand the problem better. I found peer feedback extremely useful as it gave me different perspectives to the problem that I was addressing. It was during these sessions that I realised that this problem is evident around the world and something has to be done about it. In order to make my divergent solutions more realistic, I sketched it all out. I started with a single solution and then progressed to 3 solutions and ended up with 8 different solutions.



    Finding a good solution

    This was an iterative process and involved storyboarding, brainstorming and design critique sessions, persona creation, user research and testing and refining solutions based on user feedback. At the end of this stage, I had a better idea of what my users thought the problem was and something that echoed out loud was "You are not your own user always." "Listen to the user." "There is no right answer."





    This was followed by brainstorming sessions which involved peer design critique and feedback. Throughout the design process, there was peer discussion to understand the loophooles in each other's design and this design critique was extremely useful to refine the solution. Additionally, the ethnically diverse peer feedback team helped me gain deep cultural insights from people around the world.
