Accessiblity Go Design Jam
Using gamification to make campuses more accessible

Description

Accessibility Go, inspired by Pokemon Go, is an app that that encourages empathy by challenging students to navigate the campus using accessible-only pathways, both in and outdoors, thereby bringing in an augmented reality experience to awareness programs. Users can also report problems and accessibility barriers.

Details

  • When: Aug 2016
  • Client: University of Michigan School of Information
  • Role: UX Designer
  • Project Type: Team of 4
  • Time frame: 2 days

Problem

Community members with disabilities face significant challenges on campus. In particular, those with mobility disabilities must grapple with a campus that isnâ€™t designed for them to move easily around, and it can be tough for others to change how they see the campus and the challenges it presents.

Accessibility Go! from Pallavi Gupta on Vimeo.

Target Solution

In order to cultivate more empathy from fellow peers about living with a disability, we are offering a platform that simulates a day in the life of a person with physical (later, invisible) disabilities. Using mobile technology, community members can challenge themselves to navigate the campus without using non-accessible structures, like stairs. Virtual and augmented reality will supplement the challenge experience by superimposing limitations on the mobile device.

Eventually, the application will also provide an interface for reporting accessibility problems. Problem reports will be collated and used as data to argue for structural changes by the university. Participation will be incentivized through in-game rewards, with the possibility of real-world rewards if proven effective.

Process

Problem Scoping

All the teams had to create a one minute pitch for an informational tool, service, or collection related to a social movement.

Brainstorming

We brainstormed what a social movement means to each of us and what we could work on as a team.

Identifying Solutions

We conducted some preliminary research and discussed social movements relevant to our personal passions and interests. At the end of the first day of the challenge, through structured ideation and contextual research, our team started converging on a social movement to address.

Detailing

Our solution focused on promoting awareness and empathy on university campuses
