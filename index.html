About Me



Today, the way a human interacts with a machine has radically changed. However, the user’s frustration with technology continues to increase. Don Norman’s emphasis on the need for building devices with good conceptual models illustrates how even the simplest of solutions are becoming intricately complex to design. Such thoughts have resulted in the emergence of new fields that seek to bring order to chaos by adopting the best practices of various disciplines.

As a developer, I wanted to understand user experience and human behavior. So I decided to go back to school again. Currently, I am a grad student at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor specializing in Human Computer Interaction. I am interested in Interaction Design, User Interfaces, Ubiquitous and Social Computing.
