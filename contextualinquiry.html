Realising Corporate Makerspaces Contextual Inquiry

Project Description

Our team interviewed Pillar employees to find out the current situation with their unrealized makerspace: what the employees expect out of it and what challenges they have faced in realizing it. We focused on interviews, findings, and possible recommendations on how Pillar Technology can effectively meet their expectations in utilizing a makerspace.

Project Details

  • When: Sept 2016 to present
  • Client: Pillar Technology, Ann Arbor
  • Role: UX Researcher and Consultant
  • Project Type: Team of 4
  • Time frame: 12 weeks

Problem

Pillar Technology has been in the space of crafting digital experiences for its clients for close to two decades now. The company has an open office space culture and frequently host a lot of community events. Our project revolved around their office space in downtown Ann Arbor called The Forge. Though the company actively engages the community, they believe that there is so much more potential that is untapped. When it moved to this downtown location two years ago, the company had plans to found a makerspace that have since gone unrealized.

Target Solution

As a part of this project, we conducted interviews to identify painpoints and come up with possible finding-recommendation pairs.
