Realising Corporate Makerspaces Contextual Inquiry
Addressing physical spaces in corporates to foster innovation



Description

Our team interviewed Pillar employees to find out the current situation with their unrealized makerspace: what the employees expect out of it and what challenges they have faced in realizing it. We focused on interviews, findings, and possible recommendations on how Pillar Technology can effectively meet their expectations in utilizing a makerspace.

Details

  • When: Sept 2016 to Dec 2016
  • Client: The Forge by Pillar Technology, Ann Arbor
  • Role: UX Researcher and Consultant
  • Project Type: Team of 4
  • Time frame: 12 weeks

Problem

Pillar Technology has been in the space of crafting digital experiences for its clients for close to two decades now. The company has an open office space culture and frequently hosts a lot of community events. Our project revolved around their office space in downtown Ann Arbor called The Forge. Though the company actively engages the community, they believe that there is so much more potential that is untapped. When it moved to this downtown location two years ago, the company had plans to found a makerspace that have since gone unrealized.

Target Solution

As a part of this project, we conducted interviews to identify painpoints and come up with possible finding-recommendation pairs.

Process

Methodology

Contextual inquiry is a research method that is structured around user interviews conducted at users' workplace, with the goal of obtaining a more complete sense of the strengths of weaknesses of a process or system. The information gathered in these interviews can then be interpreted to gain unique insights.

The goal of our initial meeting is to pinpoint a specific problem that has to be addressed. To understand the scope of the problem, we had another meeting. We then conducted background research on Pillar Technology and the concept of makerspaces in general. Additionally, we conducted a competitive analysis to understand makerspaces in academic versus corporate settings. The next step in the process was conducting user interviews. We interviewed around 7 employees at Pillar to gauge the problem from different perspectives. The employees interviewed were across job roles and we had a clearer picture about what employees wanted.

Affinity Wall Building

Using notes from the interview and respective interpretation sessions, we built our affinity wall. The affinity wall building process was a long one and took us roughly 10 hours, after which we gained significant insights. We used the insights gained from our prior research and interviews to identify a set of recommendations for the findings about the problem at Pillar.





Findings and Recommendations

After a 10 hour affinity wall building followed by a brainstorming session, we identified a set of key finding-recommendation pairs. We also submitted a detailed project report to our client, The Forge by Pillar Technology.
